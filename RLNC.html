Advantages of RLNC (Random Linear Network Coding)

1. What is RLNC?

RLNC allows intermediate nodes in a network to not just forward data, but to recode it. This ensures resilience to losses and high scalability in decentralized networks.

2. Comparison of Codes: RLNC vs RS vs Fountain

Code Scalability Support Recoding Complexity Use Cases
Reed-Solomon ✘ (drops with more nodes) ✘ High Storage, QR
Fountain Codes ✘ ✘ Medium IPTV, Streaming
RLNC ✔ (constant throughput) ✔ Low (distributed) Mesh, IoT, 5G

4. Why It Matters

Unlike other approaches, RLNC scales without losses, even as the number of hops increases. It ensures maximum network utilization, minimizes delays, and eliminates congestion.

5. Adaptability of RLNC

Suitable for dynamic networks: mesh networks, decentralized applications, and intermittent connections. No synchronization is required — coding and recoding happen locally and independently.
